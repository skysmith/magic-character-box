## Simple Post

I’ve been building a tiny Toniebox-style music and story box as an open-source Raspberry Pi project.

The idea is simple: put an NFC-tagged character or token on top of the box, and it plays that character’s songs, stories, sound effects, or family voice messages.

The fun part is that it is not just a speaker. It can become a Dad token, Grandma story token, dinosaur facts token, bedtime token, birthday token, or whatever a kid invents next.

Under the hood:

- Raspberry Pi Zero 2 W
- PN532 NFC reader
- MAX98357A amp and passive speaker
- Python playback service
- Local web admin for recording messages, uploading audio, registering tags, and testing playback
- 3D printable NFC figure bases

I’m turning it into an Apache-2.0 open-source tutorial so other parents, makerspaces, and STEM families can build their own version.

The goal is not a polished product. The goal is a magical little physical interface that a kid can understand immediately: choose a character, place it on the box, and the box comes alive.
